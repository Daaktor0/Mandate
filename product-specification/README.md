# Mandate — Product and Build Specification Pack

**Product name:** Mandate  
**Flagship report:** Mandate Brief  
**Product category:** AI-assisted transaction preparation  
**Primary market:** Indian corporate transaction lawyers  
**Specification date:** 13 July 2026

## Product in one sentence

Mandate converts a company website or legal name into a source-backed, editable **Mandate Brief** that helps a transaction lawyer understand the legal entity, business, industry, regulatory environment, public-risk signals and the questions that should be asked on the kickoff call.

## Core promise

> Enter a company website or legal name. Confirm the correct legal entity. Receive a concise, source-backed Mandate Brief and a tailored kickoff-call question set.

Mandate is **not** a legal-due-diligence substitute, legal opinion or confidential data room. The MVP researches public information only.

## Brand architecture

- **Mandate** — the software product and platform.
- **Mandate Brief** — the flagship source-backed report generated by Mandate.
- Future outputs may include regulatory briefs, counterparty briefs, sector briefs and matter updates without changing the product name.

## Frozen MVP decisions

- Users: individual partners and associates; law-firm workspaces come later.
- Authentication: Google and Microsoft/Outlook.
- Entity scope: Indian private companies, Indian public unlisted companies and Indian listed companies.
- Transaction context: Indian and cross-border transactions involving the supported target entity.
- Minimum initial input: company website or legal name; CIN optional.
- Legal-entity confirmation is mandatory before substantive research.
- Base research is universal; client role and transaction context change prioritisation and questions, not the underlying factual research.
- Confidential mandate details and documents are prohibited in the MVP.
- The standard Mandate Brief is two pages; an automatic length controller may produce one to four main pages.
- Sources appear in a source section/annex rather than as visible inline URLs.
- Users may edit a Mandate Brief and apply a firm letterhead before download.
- Reports and edits are versioned.
- AI learning from edits is explicit, anonymised and opt-in.
- Failed generations do not consume a report entitlement.
- The core workflow is code-based and queue-driven; n8n is not in the critical path.
- Prototype deployment: frontend + Supabase + queued worker on Hostinger KVM 2 + OpenRouter.
- Architecture remains portable to AWS Mumbai.
- A smaller Hugging Face model is a later optimisation layer.
