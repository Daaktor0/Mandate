# 07 — Mandate Brief Content and Generation Specification

## Objective

The Mandate Brief prepares a lawyer for a kickoff call. It is not a diligence report.

## Output bundle

1. one-to-four-page main Mandate Brief;
2. source section/annex;
3. editable web version;
4. PDF;
5. provenance map;
6. generation metadata;
7. version history.

## Length controller

- **Default:** two pages.
- **One page:** genuinely sparse information or a simple business; do not pad.
- **Three pages:** material multi-entity, regulatory, listed-company or cross-border complexity.
- **Four pages:** maximum for unusually complex but bounded cases.

The source annex is outside the cap and may be appended, separate or online.

## Mandate Brief structure

### Header

Legal name, brand if relevant, CIN, type/listed status, registered office, research date, client role and “Public-information transaction preparation brief.”

### A. Executive snapshot

Maximum six bullets: business, model, geography, ownership/funding, regulatory character and highest-value issue/gap.

### B. Business and operating footprint

Products/services, revenue model, customers, divisions, offices/stores/warehouses/factories, employees, assets/IP, material partners and related entities.

### C. Industry and competitors

Industry definition, value-chain position, relevant trends, direct competitors with rationale and basis of competition. Avoid filler.

### D. Corporate, people and capital

Incorporation/status, directors, management, promoters/founders, investors, funding, shareholding signals, group, listed disclosures and charges where reliable.

### E. Preliminary regulatory landscape

Sector/activity, licences, FDI/FEMA questions, automatic/approval-route indicators requiring confirmation, DPIIT/startup signals, labour thresholds, premises/factory/environmental, data/consumer and securities rules. No final legal conclusion from incomplete public facts.

### F. Matters for attention

Identity/group ambiguity, conflicts, missing operational facts, public proceedings, recent news, licensing uncertainty, company-claim reliance and early verification needs.

### G. Kickoff-call questions

Mandatory, specific, prioritised and linked to a public-information gap. Target 8–15 questions; up to 20 for complex Mandate Briefs.

## Role overlays

- **Company/promoter:** objective/timeline, responsible people, group/operations, investors/governance, cap table, locations, workforce, assets/IP, licences, financing, records, approvals and consents.
- **Investor/acquirer:** diligence perimeter, entities, employees, factories, premises, assets/IP, licences, foreign investment, contracts, related parties, litigation, borrowings, data/privacy, environment and labour.
- **Seller/transferor:** securities/title, encumbrances, transfer restrictions, shareholder rights, approvals, buyer profile, readiness and transition obligations.

## Recommendations policy

Allowed: suggested questions, verification steps, diligence focus and specialist-counsel flags. Not allowed: formal legal opinion, compliance certification, definitive regulatory status or investment recommendation.

## Source section

Each entry includes title, publisher, date, accessed date, link in web output and section supported. Raw URLs stay out of the main narrative.

## Editing and letterhead

Preserve immutable system draft and sources. Warn in the editor when user-added factual text has no system source. Apply one-page PDF/image letterhead as background/header, preview margins and continuation pages, never send it to AI and delete the temporary asset by default.

## Disclaimer

> This Mandate Brief is an AI-assisted transaction preparation brief based solely on publicly available information identified as of the research date. It is not legal advice, a legal opinion, a due-diligence report, or a representation that the information is complete or current. Public sources may be inaccurate, incomplete or delayed. All material information and preliminary regulatory observations should be independently verified.

## Quality gates

Correct entity, 1–4 pages, evidence for all material system claims, proper uncertainty, role-relevant questions, source annex, no prohibited data, disclaimer and clean PDF rendering.
