Mandate — Voice & Naming
Voice & Naming · one page
How Mandate speaks.

Calm, precise, senior. Say what is known, name what is not, never inflate. The voice of a well-prepared associate — not a chatbot, not a marketer.

Naming rules
MandateThe product. Always alone: "Sign in to Mandate", "Mandate researches public sources." Mandate BriefThe report. Both words, both capitalised, every time. Never"brief" alone in headings · "MandateBrief" · "a Mandate" for the report · "briefs" as a product noun in UI chrome. Fixed headings"Matters for attention" · "Executive snapshot" · "Suggested kickoff-call questions" · "Information not publicly established" — verbatim, not paraphrased. EntitlementsCounted as Mandate Briefs: "3 available · 1 reserved". Packs are "5 Mandate Briefs", never bundles of credits.
Sample phrasing
"Create a Mandate Brief" — button, title case only for the product terms.
"Your Mandate Brief is ready." — email subject and dashboard toast.
"Nothing has been charged." — every pre-confirmation screen.
"Your entitlement has been restored — nothing was used." — failure state.
"Confirm the company — the Mandate Brief is only reserved after you confirm."
Uncertainty phrasing — the epistemic register
✓ Verified factState it plainly: "Incorporated 12 March 2019 in Maharashtra." “” Company claim"The company states…" / "…per the company's careers page." Attribute, never adopt. ◇ Third-party report"Press reporting indicates…" — name the publisher in the annex. ~ Inference"…suggests" / "inference from absence" — always flagged as reasoning, not record. ≠ ConflictPresent both: "the website states 24,000 MT; a 2024 interview reported 18,000 MT." — Not public"Not publicly established" — the fixed phrase. Never "unknown", never silence.
Banned vocabulary
tokens credits AI lawyer due-diligence replacement guaranteed coverage complete picture instant legal insight supercharge
Also avoid: exclamation marks in product copy · urgency framing ("only 2 left!") · "magic", "smart", sparkle metaphors · percentages on progress · any sentence that implies exhaustive coverage. When in doubt, write the sentence a careful associate would sign.
