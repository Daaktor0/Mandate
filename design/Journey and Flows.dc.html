Mandate — User Journey & Screen Flows
User Journey & Screen Flows · doc-03 primary flow
From company name to kickoff call.

Six phases, every screen-to-screen transition with its initiator and dashboard status, and all ten branch/error paths designed. The three trust moments — the wrong-entity fear, the generation wait, and the failure moment — each get an explicit system response.

A · Journey map

PHASE 1
Arrive & decide to trust
Screens
Landing · Sign-in (Google/Microsoft) · First-login profile
Goal
Understand what this is — and what it is not — before typing a client's name anywhere.
"Another AI tool overpromising to lawyers?"
Mandate's response: public-information-only explanation, sample Mandate Brief on the landing page, not-legal-advice statement above the fold. No agent diagrams, no coverage claims.
Status: —
PHASE 2
Start a Mandate Brief
Screens
Dashboard · New Mandate Brief (Website / Legal name tabs)
Goal
Point Mandate at the counterparty in seconds, between calls.
"Am I allowed to type this here?"
Mandate's response: the confidentiality confirmation is a prominent, mandatory checkbox — not fine print. "No charge until you confirm the company" stated at the button.
Status: — → Entity confirmation required
PHASE 3
Confirm the company
Screens
Entity discovery (system) · Entity confirmation
Goal
Be certain the report will be about the right legal entity.
"There are six companies with this name. Wrong one = useless brief."
Mandate's response: evidence-first candidate cards (CIN, registered office, domain relationship, snippets) with shape-coded confidence labels. "None of these" is a first-class action. Nothing is reserved until the user confirms.
Status: Entity confirmation required
PHASE 4
Scope & commit
Screens
Clarification (client role) · Sparse-data disclosure · Entitlement reserve / purchase
Goal
Know exactly what will be produced and what it costs before committing.
"What am I spending, and on what, exactly?"
Mandate's response: client-role question with a "why this matters" explainer; sparse public data disclosed before anything is reserved; reserve shown as 2 available → 1 reserved. Zero balance → purchase, then return to this exact point.
Status: Clarification required → Queued
PHASE 5
The wait
Screens
Generation progress (7 truthful stages) · Ready notification (email + dashboard)
Goal
Get on with other work, confident the job is really moving.
"Is it actually doing anything, or is this a fake spinner?"
Mandate's response: a timestamped checklist of real stages — no percentages, no fake motion. Retries shown honestly ("retry scheduled 14:32"). "You can close this page; we'll email you."
Status: Queued → Researching · Verifying · Drafting · Preparing Mandate Brief → Ready
PHASE 6
Deliver & use
Screens
Brief editor · Letterhead · Preview & download · Issue reporting · Repeat purchase
Goal
Hand a partner-grade document across the table — on firm letterhead.
"Can I put my name on this?"
Mandate's response: every claim traceable in the source panel; user-added text that outruns the sources is flagged inline; letterhead-safe margins; the download sheet restates version, entity, CIN, research date and the disclaimer.
Status: Ready
